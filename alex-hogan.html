Who is Alex Hogan? Age, Biography, Net Worth, Height, Husband, Parents, Wiki

August 2024 · 4 minute read

Alex Hogan is a distinguished journalist and regional Emmy award winner who made a significant career move in 2020 by joining FOX News as a network correspondent.

Before securing her role at FOX, she worked as a TV news reporter at WFMZ-TV, where she dedicated three years and four months of her career, from September 2016 to June 2019.

With a proven track record as a reporter, Alex Hogan’s career took an exciting turn when she became widely recognized for her coverage of the funeral of Queen Elizabeth II in September 2022.

Her talent and dedication in the field of journalism have earned her accolades, including a regional Emmy award. Keep on reading to find out more about Alex Hogan.

Profile Summary

Alex Hogan Biography

Alex Hogan comes from Switzerland in Central Europe, but we don’t know exactly when she was born because she hasn’t shared that information publicly.

Judging from how she looks, it seems like she might be in her late 20s or perhaps in her 30s.

Her full name is Alexandra Hogan, and she can speak multiple languages. She’s lived in different places, including Spain, Israel, and seven states in the United States.

In terms of family, Hogan’s parents are Alex Stacie Hogan and Jim Hogan. She also has two wonderful siblings named Chris Hogan and Nick Hogan.

For education, Hogan went to Pennsylvania State University (2012 to 2016) and graduated with a bachelor’s degree in broadcast journalism.

While at the Donald P. Bellisario College of Communications there, she received a regional Emmy award for her work.

Professional Career

Alex Hogan’s journey in journalism has taken her through various roles and experiences. It all began in 2013 when she assisted at the ESPYS Awards for ESPN, helping celebrities and athletes on the red carpet.

She then moved on to become a Producer and Reporter at Penn State THON, organizing a live webcast for the world’s largest philanthropic event.

Transitioning into radio, Alex worked as a Radio News Reporter at CommRadio, producing weekly shows on local, state, and international news.

She also explored technology and management as an IT Director at Kappa Alpha Theta Indianapolis Alumnae Chapter, managing social media and technical issues.

Adding a writing dimension, Alex contributed as a Writer for Spoon University, sharing weekly articles on an online food blog.

Venturing into mainstream news, Alex served as a Newsroom Intern at NBC News from August 2014 to December 2014.

In this role, she worked in the newsroom and in the field, gaining exposure to court hearings, debates, and significant events like the Concert for Valor and the Navy Yard shooting remembrance ceremony.

Expanding her skills, Alex interned as an Assignment Desk Intern at Bloomberg LP, working on international television productions. She later became an Anchor, Reporter, and Producer at Centre County Report, contributing to a weekly live TV newscast.

In the television reporting domain, Alex served as a Multimedia Journalist at WTAJ-TV, and later as a Television News Reporter at WFMZ-TV, reporting news in Reading, Pennsylvania. She continued her career as a Network Correspondent in New York, where she has been for over four years.

Elevating her career, Alex assumed the role of a Network Correspondent for FOX News Media in New York, New York, from January 2020 to Oct 2021. She has over four years of experience in this role.

Taking her expertise global, Alex began working as a London-based, Foreign Correspondent in October for FOX News Media.
