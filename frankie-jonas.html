Frankie Jonas is an American actor and singer who is well-known for being the youngest of the Jonas siblings and is, hence, often referred to as Bonus Jonas. He has appeared in several shows and movies such as Jonas Brothers: Living the Dream, Katelyn Tarver TV, Entertainment Tonight, Disney 365, The Joey & Elise Show, TinaQ’s Celebrity Interviews, Good Morning America, Jonas, Camp Rock 2: The Final Jam, Night at the Museum: Battle of the Smithsonian, R.L. Stine’s The Haunting Hour, and Gutsy Frog. Frankie Jonas has branched out as a singer as well and has also shown his skills as a voice artist by working on projects such as The Reef 2: High Tide, Spooky Buddies, and Ponyo. Moreover, he has garnered an impressive social media fan base with more than 800k followers on Twitter and more than 700k followers on Instagram.

    As a Singer

    In 2009, Frankie Jonas sang the song, Ponyo On the Cliff By the Sea, alongside Noah Cyrus which was a part of the English version of the Japanese animated film, Ponyo.

    In February 2023, Frankie released his debut single titled Cocaine.

    First Film

    As a voiceover artist, Frankie Jonas made his theatrical film debut by providing vocals to the character of Sôsuke in the English version of the Japanese animated fantasy film, Ponyo, in 2009. The cast of the original film starred the likes of Tomoko Yamaguchi, Kazushige Nagashima, Yūki Amami, George Tokoro, Yuria Nara, Hiroki Doi, and several more.

    In 2009, Frankie Jonas made his first theatrical film appearance by playing the role of an Extra – Child in Museum in the adventure fantasy comedy film, Night at the Museum: Battle of the Smithsonian, whose lead cast included the likes of Ben Stiller, Amy Adams, Owen Wilson, Hank Azaria, Christopher Guest, Alain Chabat, and Robin Williams. However, Frankie Jonas’ part went uncredited.

    Frankie Jonas made his first credited theatrical film appearance as ‘Himself’ in the concert film, Jonas Brothers: The 3D Concert Experience, in 2010. The lead actors of the film were Kevin Jonas, Joe Jonas, Nick Jonas, Demi Lovato, Taylor Swift, and Robert “Big Rob” Feggans.

    First TV Show

    Frankie Jonas made his first TV show appearance as ‘Himself’ in an episode, titled To Do List, of the reality television series, Jonas Brothers: Living the Dream, in May 2008.
