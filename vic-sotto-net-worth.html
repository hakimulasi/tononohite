Vic Sotto Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Background

Vic Sotto, or Marvic Valentin Castelo Sotto, embarked on his journey in Manila, Philippines. Born to Marcelino Antonio Ojeda Sotto and Herminia Castelo, Vic grew up with siblings who would also make their mark in Philippine Cinema.

His education at Colegio de San Juan de Letran set the stage for a multifaceted career ahead.

Vic Sotto’s Illustrious Career Journey

Musical Beginnings and Television Breakthrough

Vic Sotto’s foray into the world of entertainment began with music. His early career as a folk singer and guitarist set the tone for his artistic versatility. It was his involvement in the disco funk band VST & Company that brought him into the limelight.

The band, known for its infectious beats and catchy melodies, popularized songs like Awitin Mo at Isasayaw Ko, resonating deeply with Filipino music lovers. This musical journey was just the start of Vic’s multifaceted career.

Transitioning to television, Vic joined his brothers Tito and Val in the gag show OK Lang, which paved the way for his future success. It was here that the famous TVJ trio, consisting of Tito Sotto, Vic, and Joey de Leon, was formed.

Their unique brand of humor and chemistry made them beloved figures in Philippine television.

Rising to Stardom with Eat Bulaga!

Vic’s journey took a significant turn when he began co-hosting Eat Bulaga!, a noontime variety show that would become a cornerstone of Filipino television.

His natural charisma and comic timing endeared him to millions of viewers, making Eat Bulaga! a household name and an enduring part of Filipino culture.

For many years, Vic, alongside his co-hosts, brought laughter and joy to viewers, making the show a daily staple in homes across the Philippines.

Filmography: A Box Office Phenomenon

Vic Sotto’s transition to the big screen saw him taking on roles that highlighted his comic talent and relatability. His involvement in the Enteng Kabisote film series was a monumental success, showcasing his ability to lead a box office hit.

Films like My Little Bossings further cemented his status as a major player in the Filipino film industry. These cinematic ventures demonstrated his range as an actor, adept in both comedic and dramatic roles.

Throughout his career, Vic has shown a remarkable ability to connect with diverse audiences, a trait that has made him a beloved figure in the Philippine Entertainment industry. His numerous accolades, including being titled the Box Office King, are a testament to his impact on Filipino cinema.
