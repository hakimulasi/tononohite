Pippa Middleton's wedding with James Matthews had subtle links to sister Kate best photos

July 2024 · 4 minute read

The couple got married in 2017

    Following her sister Kate Middleton's high-profile royal wedding six years earlier, it comes as no surprise that Pippa Middleton has been compared to the Duchess of Cambridge on her big day.

    RELATED: Kate Middleton's wedding dress was overflowing with family tributes

    The mother-of-two – who now shares son Arthur and daughter Grace with her husband – married James Matthews at St Mark's Church in Englefield on 20 May 2017, with their nephew Prince George and niece Princess Charlotte as part of their bridal party. 

    From Pippa's elegant lace dress to her sentimental earrings, it's safe to say that her regal nuptials had a couple of sweet links to her sister – but the day was just as memorable in its own right. In honour of their fifth wedding anniversary, look back at Pippa and James' best photos.

    WATCH: Best moments from Pippa Middleton and James Matthews' romantic wedding

    Pippa wore a floor-length Giles Deacon dress, with a high neckline and fitted waist. She finished off her bridal look with Manolo Blahnik pumps and a handmade Maidenhair Fern tiara with a matching hairpiece by Robinson Pelham.

    Her ultra-fine tulle veil was designed by milliner Stephen Jones and encrusted with pearls. The beautiful bride wore the same pair of delicate diamond drop Robinson Pelham earrings that were commissioned for the Duchess of Cambridge's wedding in 2011.

    Mother-of-the-bride Carole Middleton was pretty in pink as she arrived on the arm of son James Middleton.

    The beautiful bride stopped to smile at well-wishers gathered at St. Mark's church for her special day. 

    The Duchess of Cambridge helped the bride adjust her train – just as Pippa had done for her during her royal wedding in 2011.
