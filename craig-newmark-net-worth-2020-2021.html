Career

Following his graduation, Craig had joined the prestigious company of IBM, where he had officially served as a programmer for seventeen long years. This had encouraged several people to actively participate in the technical posts, which had led to the establishment of the “Jobs” section. With the inclusion of newer facilities and welcoming fresh recruiters as per the requirement, Craig went on to create his own website in the year 1996, which was henceforth known as Craigslist.org.

In 1998, he had also furnished himself with additional knowledge of Java programming and had started serving as a software engineer. With the unstoppable growth and popularity of Craigslist in 1999, Craig had started contributing to the website on a full-time basis.

In 2004, Craig had also included a separate section where paid jobs could be posted. Besides this, one could also post unpaid jobs, free internships, and low-paid recruitment options in the “Gigs” section.

By 2012, Craigslist had already spread to around 700 other cities within 70 major countries. Reaching over 100 million advertisements, more than 6 million job postings, and around 30 million new views, Craigslist had scaled new milestones and is ever-growing to date.
