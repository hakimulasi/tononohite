Tim Harmston Full Overview and Wiki

From Menomonie to Minneapolis: The Early Years

Born in the early ’70s in Wisconsin, Harmston’s journey into comedy wasn’t a straight path. Originally from Menomonie, Harmston’s early career saw him working in the Chicago film industry.

This eclectic background laid a unique foundation for his eventual pivot to stand-up comedy in 2002, blending observational humor with a keen eye for the absurdity of everyday life.

The Rise on the Comedy Scene

His comedic career truly began to take shape after he started performing stand-up in 2002.

His breakthrough came quickly, winning Minneapolis comedy club Acme’s Funniest Person in the Twin Cities contest in 2003.

This victory was not just a local accolade; it signaled the emergence of a comedic voice that was both insightful and delightfully absurd, drawing comparisons to industry stalwarts like Brian Regan and Jim Gaffigan for his unique cadence and observational style.

Competitions like Last Comic Standing in 2014 and appearances on The Late Show With David Letterman and Comedy Central’s Live at Gotham underscored his growing prominence in the comedy world.

Albums, Accolades, and Aspen

In 2011, he self-released his debut album, The Most Bees Ever, showcasing his originality and solidifying his place in the comedy world.

This album, later re-released by Stand Up! Records in 2014, received praise for its unique take on stand-up, compared by some to the work of Steven Wright.

His follow-up album, The Whim of Tim, released in 2020, continued to build on his unique approach to comedy.

Beyond albums, Harmston’s inventive mind led to the creation of the award-winning internet video series Chad Vader: Day Shift Manager, further demonstrating his versatility and creative depth.

His recognition also included winning a Rusty Nail award at the Aspen RooftopComedy Festival in 2008, proving his comedic talents were not limited to just stand-up but spanned across various media.

A Comedic Partnership

His personal life, especially his marriage to fellow comedian Mary Mack, has been a source of both support and collaboration.

The duo frequently tours together, combining their comedic talents to the delight of audiences nationwide.
