Ian Harris Full Overview and Wiki

The Man Behind the Laughter and Punches

Born on the sunny shores of Santa Cruz, California, Ian Harris has carved a niche for himself in the realms of comedy, directing, and mixed martial arts.

This American national has traversed a journey from swinging punches to delivering punchlines, making him a standout figure in the entertainment and fitness industries.

A Comedic Journey of Skepticism and Laughter

His comedy career took off in the early ’90s when he moved to Los Angeles. His initial foray into the world of laughs didn’t immediately lead to success, but his persistence paid off.

He quickly became known for his brash, irreverent humor, eventually taking a brief hiatus only to return with a vengeance, integrating skepticism and critical thinking into his act.

His comedy specials, Critical & Thinking and ExtraOrdinary, showcase his unique blend of humor and intellect, contributing to his reputation and presumably, his financial success.

Directing and Beyond

Not content to stay within the confines of comedy, Harris expanded his horizons into directing.

He’s helmed comedy specials for other comedians, such as Dwayne Perkins, and created a slew of films and short movies.

This diversification undoubtedly added depth to his career and breadth to his income streams.

The Fighter and the Trainer

Parallel to his comedy career, Harris has been deeply involved in martial arts, training in various styles from a young age.

His establishment of Fight Science, an MMA training center in Los Angeles, not only capitalized on his fighting skills but also offered a unique blend of fight training with a scientific approach.

This innovative style likely contributes significantly to his income, especially in the burgeoning world of MMA.

Personal Life

Harris embodies the spirit of the 80s punk rock and counter-culture.

A product of San Lorenzo Valley High School, his early interests spanned stand-up comedy, writing, boxing, martial arts, and surfing.

A self-proclaimed atheist, his comedic acts resonate with a particular demographic: older, educated, and similarly secular-minded individuals who appreciate the punk rock essence of his performances.

Residing in Los Angeles with his wife Jeaninne and their daughter Bella, Harris advocates for respect and unity within the diverse secular community, emphasizing atheism’s broad spectrum of political beliefs.

What is Fight Science?

Fight Science is a Los Angeles-based MMA training center owned by Ian Patrick Harris. It is named after the unique fighting style that he created, which integrates skepticism and critical thinking into his comedy.

Has he won any awards?

Yes, he has received several accolades throughout his career, including Best Music at the 2004 48 Hour Film Project in Los Angeles, Finalist at the 2003 Fade-In Magazine Short Film Awards, Best Film at the 2003 Zephyr Short Film Festival in Los Angeles, and the Audience Choice Award at the 2002 Colfax Film Festival.

What is The Iron Boot Scrapers?

The Iron Boot Scrapers is an Edwardian rock band created by Ian Harris. The band is known for its unique blend of modern rock instruments with antiquated instruments like the sousaphone and brass, creating what can be described as Edwardian Rock.

Where is he from?

He was born in Banbury. His father worked in the Foreign Office, leading to a childhood spent in various locations, including Detroit in the 60s and later on in Israel.

What inspired he to create a stilt walking band?

The inspiration for creating a stilt walking band came from Ian’s desire to increase his busking takes.

He started busking as a giant Mr. Punch character and later suggested the idea of a stilt walking band to an entertainment agency partner, which turned out to be a successful venture.

What are some of his musical influences?

His musical influences include Tom Waits, The Tiger Lillies, Motorhead, The Electric Light Orchestra, and AC/DC. He also drew inspiration from the street band Swervy World for his busking bands.
