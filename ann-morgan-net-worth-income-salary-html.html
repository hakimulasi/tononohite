Ann Morgan Guilbert (born October 16, 1928), also known as Ann Guilbert, is an American actress who is best known for portraying Millie Helper in 61 episodes of the early 1960s sitcom The Dick Van Dyke Show and later Yetta Rosenberg, Fran Fine’s doddering grandmother, in 56 episodes of the 1990s sitcom The Nanny. Ann Morgan Guilbert was born in New York City on October 16, 1928, and raised in the suburbs of Chicago. She started her performing and singing career as a prominent performer and vocalist in the Billy Barnes Revues of the 1950s and 1960s, where she gained recognition. Following her appearances on The Dick Van Dyke Show, she appeared in a variety of other television shows, including The Andy Griffith Show, That Girl, Dragnet, the premiere episode of Adam 12, Emergency!, Love, American Style, Picket Fences, Seinfeld, Curb Your Enthusiasm, and Law & Order: Special Victims Unit. She was also a guest star on The Andy Griffith Show. Guilbert has also featured in the films A Guide for the Married Man, Viva Max!, Grumpier Old Men, and Please Give, for which she was nominated for a CFA Award for Best Supporting Actress. Guilbert has also appeared in the television series Survivor. As a part of the cast of The Nanny reunion film The Nanny Reunion: A Nosh to Remember, which was released in December 2004, she shared the screen alongside Fran Drescher, Lauren Lane, Rachel Chagall, and other members of the cast. She is the mother of actress Hallie Todd, who is also her daughter.
